# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have compiled this checklist to help peers maintain a professional and safe online presence.

| Category | Do's | Don'ts |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Privacy & Security** | Use strong privacy settings to control who sees your personal posts. | Don't post sensitive data like your home address, phone number, or OTPs. |
| **Professionalism** | Keep your LinkedIn profile updated with your latest projects and skills. | Don't post content that depicts illegal activities or violates college code. |
| **Engagement** | Fact-check information before sharing to avoid spreading misinformation. | Don't engage in cyberbullying or use offensive language in public or private chats. |
| **Digital Footprint** | Think "Pause Before You Post"—remember that digital records are permanent. | Don't share confidential university documents or exam materials online. |
| **Networking** | Use social media to connect with industry experts and alumni in your field. | Don't tag others in unprofessional photos without their explicit permission. |

### Summary
Responsible social media use is a key part of digital literacy. Your online behavior is a reflection of your professional character and can impact future internship and job opportunities.
